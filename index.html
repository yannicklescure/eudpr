EUDPR, seamless GDPR

You need a data protection representative in the EU if...

  • You have a website,
  • You are doing business on the Internet as a consultant, coach, business professional, service provider or retailer,
  • You are located outside the EU, and
  • You are serving clients or customers in the EU.

Required By the EU Law

Article 27 of the General Data Protection Regulation requires that you appoint a representative in the EU as your point of contact for clients, customers and authorities regarding privacy matters.

If you are a small business tucked away in a small village and your clientele are all locals, this doesn't apply to you.

But if you have clients and customers in the EU, if you want a global presence, and if you want to comply with the law, then this is not optional. You must have an Article 27 EU Representative.

Does the GDPR apply to my business?

The GDPR applies to you if you hold any data on anyone in the EU.

This means if you have any customers in the EU or if you have anyone in the EU on your list or if your website uses tracking cookies or pixels and anyone from the EU visits your site. It doesn’t matter where you are in the world, if you hold data on anyone in the EU, this applies to you and your business. It is a legal requirement and you must comply.
